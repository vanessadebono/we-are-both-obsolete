we are both obsolete reflects on the shared
obsolescence of technology and the human
condition in the digital age

about

Through a series of newly developed works, it invites critical
engagement with the psychological, social, and environmental
costs of technological progress—from the emotional toll of
digital acceleration to the material consequences of planned
obsolescence and e-waste.

The works on show span generative video, modified interfaces, sculptural assemblage, and
algorithmic sound. Some manipulate obsolete software to create surreal, degraded landscapes;
others rewire everyday devices into dysfunctional prototypes or glitch aesthetics. Throughout,
physical and digital materials are entangled, printed circuit boards sit alongside brushstrokes
while coded behaviours interrupt sculptural forms—blurring the boundary between technological
debris and artistic gesture.

Please explore the systems that make both machines and people disposable: planned obsolescence,
algorithmic burnout, fractured attention, and the quiet violence of constant upgrades. Visitors are
invited to reflect on a culture where relevance is engineered to expire by design, not by necessity.

The Malta Society of Arts was established in 1852, and is Malta’s
oldest institution for the promotion of the arts and crafts. The
MSA runs an annual programme of art exhibitions and music
concerts. It is also home to a school for education in fine art,
craft and music.
